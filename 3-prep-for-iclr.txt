A few specific positions on each:
TMLR is the strongest immediate recovery. Rolling deadline means you can submit when §4 and S019E are actually resolved rather than against an arbitrary cutoff. TMLR explicitly does not reward scalar leaderboard primacy and explicitly values benchmark + methodology contributions — that's about as aligned with C1/C2 as a venue gets. ICLR 2027 is the strongest if you want a flagship. Sept/Oct gives 4 months to clean §4, fill XXX, resolve S019E, and pivot the framing to V6-C. The risk Version C has internally (overstating impossibilities invites scrutiny) is more survivable at ICLR than NeurIPS D&B because ICLR reviewers will read Bilodeau et al. precisely and won't conflate "complete linear attribution" with "scalar reporting" if you don't.
KDD ADS 2027 is the safest if the empirics aren't fully resolved by fall. The ADS track explicitly accepts incomplete-but-deployed work, and V6-B opens on PDFM Table 4 as a real-world unexplained puzzle that practitioners hit. Lower prestige than ICLR, but the practitioner audience is closer to the people who actually run CONUS-27-shaped benchmarks.
